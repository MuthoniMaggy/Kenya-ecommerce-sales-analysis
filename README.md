# Kenya E-Commerce Sales Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes transactional data from a Kenyan online shop to uncover insights on sales performance, customer behavior, product demand, and regional trends. The goal is to generate actionable insights that can support business growth and decision-making.

---

## Objectives

- Identify top-performing products
- Analyze revenue distribution across locations
- Understand customer purchasing behavior
- Explore revenue trends over time
- Evaluate pricing and quantity relationships

---

## Tools & Technologies

- Python
- Pandas
- Matplotlib
- Seaborn
- Jupyter Notebook (VS Code)

---

## Dataset

The dataset contains transactional records with the following features:

- InvoiceNo
- CustomerID
- Product
- Quantity
- UnitPrice
- TotalPrice
- InvoiceDate
- Location
- CustomerType

---

## Key Analyses Performed

- Revenue by location
- Customer segmentation analysis
- Monthly revenue trend analysis
- Top-selling product analysis
- Pricing vs quantity relationship analysis

---

## Key Insights

### Regional Performance

- Nairobi generates the highest revenue among all locations, highlighting its importance as the primary market.
- This indicates a strong customer base and higher purchasing activity in urban areas.
- Other locations contribute relatively lower revenue, indicating potential gaps in market penetration.

### Customer Segmentation

- Premium customers have the highest average spending, making them the most valuable segment.
- Budget customers show moderate spending, slightly exceeding Regular customers, indicating potential for upselling.
- Regular customers have the lowest average spending, suggesting higher price sensitivity.
- Spending variability is highest among Premium customers, indicating inconsistent but high-value purchases.

### Revenue Trends

- Revenue peaks in the early part of the year, with the highest performance recorded in Month 1.
- A noticeable decline occurs after Month 4, indicating reduced sales activity.
- From Month 5 onwards, revenue stabilizes at a lower level with minimal fluctuations.

### Product Performance

- Fast-moving consumer goods dominate sales, with items like sanitary towels and eggs leading in quantity sold.
- These products show consistent demand and play a key role in driving transaction volume.

### Pricing Behavior

- A high concentration of transactions occurs at lower unit prices, where customers tend to purchase multiple units.
- As unit prices increase, purchases shift toward single-item transactions.

---

## Business Implications

- The business is heavily reliant on Nairobi, indicating a need to expand into underperforming regions to diversify revenue.
- Retaining Premium customers should be a priority, as they contribute significantly to overall sales.
- There is strong potential to upsell Budget and Regular customers through targeted marketing strategies.
- Seasonal fluctuations suggest the need for consistent marketing and promotional campaigns throughout the year.
- Inventory strategies should prioritize high-demand, low-cost items while optimizing stock levels for high-value products.
- Pricing strategies such as bundling and discounts can help increase purchase volumes.

---

## Conclusion

The analysis reveals that revenue is highly concentrated in Nairobi, demonstrating the business’s strong presence in urban markets. However, the relatively lower performance in other regions highlights significant growth opportunities. By leveraging customer segmentation, optimizing pricing strategies, and improving regional market penetration, the business can increase revenue consistency and overall profitability.
